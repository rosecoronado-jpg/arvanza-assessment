💡 WHY — The Strategy Behind It

Why kids toothpaste KW campaigns?
The signal: "Kids toothpaste" was already generating 134 orders/month at 29.5% CVR through existing campaigns — real, consistent demand with no dedicated structure behind it. That CVR tells us shoppers searching this term are highly purchase-ready.

The approach: Launched Exact, Phrase, and Broad for both Mint and Vanilla (×4 campaigns, Mar 26–27) to find the most efficient match type entry point. Only 4–5 days old at month end — too early to judge.

Early read (4 days): Vanilla Exact already at 48% ACoS / 2.08x ROAS / 31.9% CVR — strongest signal of the group. Broad and Phrase running hot (90–122% ACoS) as expected this early. Read date: April 25.
Why mouthwash auto-segmented TOS ×6?
The signal: "Happy tooth mouthwash" branded term was converting at 67.3% CVR and 9.7% ACoS — 10.36x ROAS — with no dedicated non-branded mouthwash structure to capture shoppers who weren't already searching by name. That gap was leaving real volume on the table.

The approach: Auto-segmented TOS structure (Substitutes / Complements / Loose / Close) separates intent types so each segment can be optimized independently rather than lumped into one auto campaign.

20-day results: Mint Substitutes 24.7% ACoS / 4.05x ROAS ✅ — best performer. Complements 17.3% ACoS / 5.79x ROAS ✅ — exceptional early signal. Orange Loose at 29.2% ACoS / 3.43x ROAS also tracking well. Blended across all 6: $433 spend → $1,454 sales / 3.36x ROAS. Hypothesis confirmed.
Why expand competitor PTs (Dr. Jens / RiseWell / SuperTeeth)?
The signal: Happy Tooth's session CVR is 50–70% on branded terms — customers who find the product buy it. The opportunity is getting in front of shoppers actively comparing hydroxyapatite products on competitor listings, where purchase intent is already high.

The approach: Targeted the 3 closest ASINs in the hydroxyapatite category — Dr. Jens, RiseWell, and SuperTeeth. Launched Mar 24, only 7 days old at month end.

7-day results: Dr. Jens 44.6% ACoS / 2.24x ROAS / 190 orders — already showing real volume. RiseWell at 84.7% ACoS / 1.18x ROAS — struggling at current bids, needs a cut. SuperTeeth: 0 orders yet — too early or wrong audience. Read date: April 23.
Why Orange Mouthwash DEF branded phrase?
The signal: As the mouthwash line grew, branded search for the orange SKU was going undefended — any competitor could show up when a customer searched "happy tooth mouthwash." That's a leaky bucket problem at the bottom of the funnel.

The approach: Launched a dedicated branded phrase defense campaign for the orange mouthwash SKU on Mar 11 to protect the conversion path for customers who already know the brand.

20-day results: $142 spend → $744 sales / 19.1% ACoS / 5.23x ROAS / 65.6% CVR. One of the highest-performing campaigns launched in March. Exactly what a branded defense should look like — low cost, high conviction customer, near-zero waste.
